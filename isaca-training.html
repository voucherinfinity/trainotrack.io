Solutions: all your questions answered

ISACA certifications, including CISA, CISM, CRISC, and CGEIT, validate expertise in IT governance, risk management, cybersecurity, and auditing. Highly respected in the industry, these credentials enhance career opportunities in various IT domains.

The salary of a Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA) in India can vary based on factors such as experience, location, employer, and specific job responsibilities. However, on average, a CISA-certified professional in India can expect to earn between INR 600,000 to INR 1,500,000 per year. Those with several years of experience and additional certifications or advanced degrees may command higher salaries, potentially exceeding this range.

Deciding between CISA and CISM boils down to career direction: CISA suits those inclined toward auditing and compliance roles, emphasizing skills in assessing vulnerabilities, ensuring compliance, and implementing controls within an organization's IT infrastructure. On the other hand, CISM is better suited for individuals aiming for leadership positions in information security management, focusing on strategic planning, risk management, and overall security program development and implementation. Consider your career aspirations and desired skill set to determine which certification aligns best with your goals.

ISACA allows candidates to take the CISA (Certified Information Systems Auditor) exam an unlimited number of times within a five-year period of the date of initially passing the exam. However, if a candidate fails the exam, there is a waiting period of 48 hours before they can re-register for another attempt. Additionally, candidates must pay the exam registration fee each time they attempt the exam.
